Collaborations



OVERVIEW


    Colorectal cancer (CRC) is a common malignant tumor of the digestive tract worldwide. It has a high incidence and fatality rate. Our project this year focuses on the early diagnosis and treatment of colorectal cancer. In the competition, we actively seek cooperation with the outside world, increase contact with universities and enterprises, and join in various aspects including team cooperation and multi-team union.In the process of collarboration, we hope to obtain suggestions from all parties,including high school students, other teams and companies, to improve our project in experimental design, experimental technology optimization, and modeling methods improvement and so on. At the same time, we hope to put forward constructive suggestions for other teams to achieve the effect of mutual benefit and create an atmosphere of mutual progress.
    In the process,we contacted with LZU-CHINA, BIT-CHINA and YiYe_China in team operation,then took part in ,meetup in Bluepha, CCiC and joint science lecture.

    Joint science lecture


       We also used other online promotions. On the Bilibili platform, team BIT and team YiYe-China conducted a live broadcast of colorectal cancer science promotion and synthetic biology promotion. The live broadcast introduced the clinical symptoms, hazards, mainstream of colorectal cancer, detection methods and more optimized detection methods conceived by the two teams respectively. This live broadcast has nearly 5,000 popularity points (approximately 500 people) watched this live broadcast. The main users of the Bilibili platform are young people aged 14-30. At the same time, the younger age and early onset of colorectal cancer are not well known to the public. Therefore, our live broadcast is aimed at the "target audience". Through this live broadcast, we can let them know more about colorectal cancer. So we can introduce our project to them and broaden the audience base of the product.
       In this live broadcast, there are also researchers who study CRC provided us with very valuable opinions during the live broadcast. His suggestions such as "the pros and cons of the current markers and the detection methods used" and "how to solve the problem of false positives" all make us think. Therefore, we also arranged special experiments to solve these problems in the follow-up project improvement problem. At the same time, he also recommended that we increase the carrier experiment, and after in-depth communication with him, we will also take this as one of our future improvement directions.
